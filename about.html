Affordable and Clean Energy

AI-Based Multi-Agent Energy Management System

Overview

This project addresses UN Sustainable Development Goal 7: Affordable and Clean Energy by developing a rule-based AI multi-agent system that optimizes energy generation, storage, distribution, consumption, maintenance, and pricing — making power systems smarter, more reliable, and sustainable.

🎯 Objective

To create an intelligent, automated energy management system that works in real-time and adapts to changing conditions (like weather, demand, pricing) without requiring machine learning.

🏗️ System Architecture

Generation Agent

Optimizes energy mix (solar, wind, hydro, gas) based on demand and weather.

Storage Agent

Manages battery charging/discharging efficiently and safely.

Distribution Agent

Prioritizes critical zones and reroutes power during faults.

Consumption Agent

Suggests energy-saving actions to reduce peak load and costs.

Maintenance Agent

Detects faults, schedules repairs, and prevents outages.

Pricing Agent

Recommends best sourcing strategies based on cost and supply.

🛠️ Technologies Used

  • Python
  • Rule-based decision logic
  • Weather & pricing data APIs
  • (Optional) Smart grid simulation tools

🧪 How It Works – Example

On a sunny, windy day:

  • Generation Agent chooses solar and wind.
  • Storage Agent ensures extra power is stored in batteries.
  • At night or in bad weather, system uses hydro or gas.
  • Distribution Agent ensures hospitals remain powered.
  • Maintenance Agent alerts technicians before breakdowns.
  • Pricing Agent adjusts sourcing to minimize cost.

Impact

  • Improved energy access and reliability
  • Reduced bills and operational costs
  • Increased renewable integration, lower emissions
  • Scalable from rural microgrids to urban networks

🚀 Future Scope

  • Add predictive analytics for forecasting
  • Partner with governments/utilities for deployment
